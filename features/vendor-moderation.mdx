The vendor moderation module gives admins control over what products appear in the catalogue and how individual sellers are managed. It consists of two tools under the **Moderación** section of the admin dashboard.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Approval queue" icon="list-check" href="/features/vendor-moderation#approval-queue">
    Review, preview, approve, or reject pending product submissions from sellers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Vendor performance" icon="chart-bar" href="/features/vendor-moderation#vendor-performance-dashboard">
    Monitor seller revenue, order counts, commission rates, and suspension status.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Approval queue

**Route:** `/admin/moderacion/aprobacion`  
**Component:** `src/pages/admin/Vetting/ApprovalQueue.jsx`

The approval queue shows every product submitted by sellers that has `status === 'pending'`. Pending products do not appear in the public catalogue until an admin approves them.

Data comes from `pendingProducts` in `MetricsContext`. After an approval decision, `refreshProducts()` from `ProductContext` is called to sync the live catalogue.

### Filtering by seller

A dropdown at the top right of the page lists all sellers who have pending submissions. Selecting a seller filters the grid to show only that seller's products. The dropdown entry for each seller shows their pending count.

### Product card grid

Pending products are displayed in a responsive card grid (2 columns on large screens, 3 on extra-large). Each card shows:

- Product image (falls back to `/placeholder.png`)
- Yellow **Pendiente** badge
- Product name, category, and truncated description
- Price in thousands (e.g. `$45k`)
- Seller name with first-letter avatar and submission date

### Actions per product

Each card has three action buttons:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ver (preview)">
    Opens a full-screen modal showing the product image, name, price, full description, and seller information. The modal also provides **Aprobar** and **Rechazar** buttons so you can make a decision without closing the preview.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Aprobar">
    Calls `approveProduct(productId)` immediately and then calls `refreshProducts()` to push the product into the live catalogue. No confirmation step.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rechazar">
    Opens the rejection modal (see below).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Rejection modal

Rejecting a product requires a written reason. This reason is stored and can be communicated back to the seller.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Rechazar">
    Either on the card or inside the preview modal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a rejection reason">
    Describe what the seller needs to fix. For example: *"Las fotos no muestran claramente el producto ni la composición de la tela."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Confirmar Rechazo**. The button is disabled until the text area is non-empty. The rejection calls `rejectProduct(productId, rejectReason)` in `MetricsContext`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The rejection reason field cannot be blank. Leaving a clear, actionable message helps sellers resubmit correctly and reduces re-review cycles.
</Warning>

### Empty state

When there are no pending products (or none match the seller filter), a **¡Todo al día!** empty state is shown. This is the desired steady state of the queue.

## Vendor performance dashboard

**Route:** `/admin/moderacion/vendedores`  
**Component:** `src/pages/admin/Vetting/VendorPerformance.jsx`

The vendor performance dashboard lists all users with `role === 'seller'` and enriches them with aggregated metrics from `getAllSellersMetrics(sellers, orders, bugReports)` in `metricsUtils`.

### Summary cards

| Card | Description |
|---|---|
| Total Vendedores | Count of all seller accounts |
| Vendedores Activos | Sellers without the `suspended` flag |
| Suspendidos | Sellers with `suspended === true` |
| Comisión Promedio | Mean `commissionRate` across all sellers |

### Sales comparison chart

A Recharts `BarChart` compares the top 5 sellers by sales volume and order count using a dual y-axis layout:

- **Left axis (purple `#8B5CF6`)** — sales in thousands of COP
- **Right axis (amber `#F59E0B`)** — order count

Seller names are truncated to the first word for readability.

### Sellers table

The full seller list is displayed in a table with these columns:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Vendedor | Avatar, name, and email |
| Ventas | Total revenue formatted as COP |
| Pedidos | Total order count (blue badge) |
| Comisión % | Editable commission rate |
| Reportes | Count of bug reports linked to this seller, with an alert icon if > 3 |
| Estado | Active (green) or Suspended (red) badge with suspension reason |
| Control | Toggle button to suspend or reactivate |

Suspended sellers appear at 50 % opacity in the table.

### Editing commission rate

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the commission value">
    Click the green percentage shown in the **Comisión %** column. The cell switches to an editable number input pre-filled with the current rate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the new rate">
    Type the new percentage (numeric). Press **Enter** or click outside the field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The new rate is saved via `updateSellerCommission(sellerId, newRate)` in `MetricsContext`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Suspending a seller

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the toggle button">
    In the **Control** column, click the red toggle icon for the seller you want to suspend.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a suspension reason">
    The suspension modal appears. Enter a reason, for example: *"Múltiples reportes de calidad de productos sin resolución."* The reason is stored on the seller record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Confirmar Suspensión**. The button is disabled until the reason field is non-empty. `toggleSellerSuspension(sellerId, reason)` is called in `MetricsContext`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Reactivating a suspended seller

For a seller that is already suspended, the toggle button switches to a green reactivation icon. Clicking it calls `toggleSellerSuspension(sellerId)` without requiring a reason, and immediately restores the seller to active status.

<Warning>
  Suspending a seller does not automatically unpublish their existing products from the catalogue. If you need to remove their listings, do so separately through the product management tools.
</Warning>
